final stats project: 

Investigating how socioeconomic conditions in high vs. low poverty regions in Africa shape electricity access.

Findings
1. Higher-poverty countries average 44.3% electrification vs 75.1% for lower-poverty countries — a ~31 percentage-point gap.
2. Urban–rural electricity access gaps are larger in medium- and high-poverty countries (~43–47%) compared to low-poverty countries (~35%).
3. Countries with 70%+ electrification have average GDP per capita 9× higher than those below 30%.
4. After controlling for development indicators, a one-unit increase in poverty rate is associated with +1,070 hours of outage duration (SAIDI) and +94 additional interruptions (SAIFI) per year.

Data
Source: CATF Africa dataset

Key variables: 
poverty_rate
electrification_rate
urban_access_electricity_rate
rural_access_electricity_rate
gdp (per capita, USD)
SAIDI, SAIFI
